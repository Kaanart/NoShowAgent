# Product Guidelines

## 1. User Experience (UX) Principles
- **Clarity & Efficiency:** Schedulers are busy. The interface must prioritize clear visualization of no-show probabilities and actionable backup tasks. Minimize clicks.
- **Data Confidence:** Predictions must be presented with understandable confidence levels. Avoid "black box" feelings by surfacing key reasons for a high no-show probability if possible.
- **Action-Oriented:** Dashboards should immediately draw attention to high-risk appointments that require manual review or patient contact.

## 2. Branding & Tone
- **Professional & Reassuring:** The tone in patient communications (SMS reminders) should be polite, professional, and clear, reflecting a high-quality hospital environment.
- **Urgent but not Alarming:** Internal alerts for high-risk no-shows should clearly indicate urgency for backfilling without causing unnecessary panic among staff.

## 3. Accessibility & Responsiveness
- **Desktop First (Staff):** The primary interface for schedulers and technicians will be on hospital desktop computers. Optimize layouts for large screens and data-dense tables.
- **Mobile Friendly (Patients):** Any patient-facing touchpoints (like SMS confirmation links) must be fully optimized for mobile devices.
